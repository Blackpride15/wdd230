Welcome to Cross River State Calabar

Welcome to Calabar, a city rich in history, culture, and business opportunities. Located in southern Nigeria, Calabar is known for its vibrant economy, historical landmarks, and lively festivals. Whether you're a business owner, investor, or visitor, Calabar offers a unique blend of tradition and modern growth.

Explore History

Calabar has a deep historical background, once serving as a major trading hub during colonial times. Today, visitors can explore sites like the Old Residency Museum, the Marina Resort, and the Slave History Museum, which tell the story of Calabar’s role in Nigerian and African history.

Culinary Delights

Beyond history, Calabar is famous for its delicious cuisine. From spicy Afang soup to fresh seafood, the city’s food scene showcases the rich flavors of Cross River State. Whether you’re dining at a local restaurant or exploring street food, you’ll experience the best of Nigerian hospitality through its meals.

Vibrant Cultures

Calabar is home to Nigeria’s biggest street festival, the Calabar Carnival, known as "Africa’s Biggest Street Party." Throughout the year, the city hosts cultural events, music festivals, and art exhibitions that celebrate the creative spirit of the people. Whether it’s a masquerade performance or a live music show, there’s always something exciting happeninge.

Outdoor Adventures

For nature lovers and adventure seekers, Calabar offers breathtaking outdoor experiences. Explore the lush rainforests of Afi Mountain Wildlife Sanctuary, visit Kwa Falls, or take a boat ride along the Calabar River. With its tropical beauty and diverse wildlife, the city is a perfect destination for relaxation and exploration.

Chamber Information

The Calabar Chamber of Commerce supports businesses by providing resources, networking opportunities, and advocacy. Whether you're starting a new business, looking to expand, or seeking investment opportunities, we are here to help.
